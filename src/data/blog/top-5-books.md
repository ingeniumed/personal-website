One of Tim Ferriss's favourite interviewing techniques is to ask his guests:

> What are the top 5 books that you gift every year?

It's a unique way to ask about someone's favourite books, because you wouldn't frequently gift a book that wasn't one. It prompted me to think about how I would answer this question. So here it is — each of these books has had an impact on my life, and I revisit them every year.

## [The Alchemist](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18144590-the-alchemist)

Paulo Coelho's story about a shepherd's journey to find his personal legend. It's a short read, but every time I pick it up I take something different from it. It's the kind of book that meets you where you are in life and reminds you to keep pursuing what matters to you.

## [The Beekeeper of Aleppo](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/43124137-the-beekeeper-of-aleppo)

A heartbreaking novel about a Syrian couple fleeing the war and making their way to Europe. It gave me a perspective on the refugee experience that news coverage never could. I gift this one because it builds empathy in a way that stays with you long after you finish it.

## [Ikigai](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/40534545-ikigai)

This book explores the Japanese concept of finding your reason for being — the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs and what you can be paid for. It's a calming read that helped me think about purpose and longevity in a more intentional way.

## [The Four Hour Work Week](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/9278897-the-4-hour-work-week)

Tim Ferriss challenges just about every assumption you have about work and productivity. Even if you don't agree with everything in it, it rewires how you think about your time. Working remotely at Automattic, a lot of the ideas in this book resonate with how I approach my day-to-day.

## [Steve Jobs](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11084145-steve-jobs)

Walter Isaacson's biography is one I re-read every few years. Jobs was deeply flawed, but his obsession with craft and simplicity is inspiring. Each time I read it at a different stage of my career, I pick up new lessons about leadership, product thinking and staying relentless about quality.
